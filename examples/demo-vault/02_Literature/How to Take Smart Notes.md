# How to Take Smart Notes

Sonke Ahrens explains the Zettelkasten method for academic writing and thinking. The core insight: writing is not the bottleneck — having a system that surfaces connections is.

## Three Note Types

1. **Fleeting notes** — quick captures, temporary (inbox)
2. **Literature notes** — summaries of sources, in your own words
3. **Permanent notes** — your own ideas, linked to existing knowledge

## Key Principles

- Write notes as if for someone else (forces clarity)
- One idea per note (atomic)
- Always link to existing notes (context)
- Let structure emerge bottom-up (no pre-planned categories)
- The slip-box is a conversation partner, not a filing cabinet
